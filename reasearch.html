The actual origins and even meanings of the phrase are as varied as the cultures that use it, with some suggesting roots in the Indo-European or Celtic belief that spirits good and bad resided in trees, which could either be invoked for protection or chased away by knocking on their homes, and others (especially Christians) associate the practice with the magical power of the wooden crucifix. Salt over your shoulder” European/Christian, Ancient Roman Perhaps the second most common superstition, at least in the West, involves throwing salt over one's shoulder. Why have your values ​​and beliefs changed?
Life Experience and Reflection: Exposure to different situations and people broadens your perspective and often leads to self-awareness. Difficult experiences make you rethink and reevaluate your values.
Cultural and social influence: Society and culture shape values ​​through norms, traditions and expectations. Exposure to different cultures and social movements can develop empathy and tolerance, leading to adjustments in values. Education and Intellectual Growth: Learning and critical thinking introduce new ideas and philosophies into educational institutions. This intellectual stimulation challenges prejudices and encourages a reassessment of values. Environment and Global Change: Environmental crises and global events cause changes in values. For example, climate change promotes values ​​that define sustainability, while political unrest or pandemics force people to rethink priorities. Personal growth and development: Of course, maturity and self-reflection lead to changes in values. People are better able to understand their own desires and thus be able to shift their own values ​​in response to their developing identities.
